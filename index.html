Regulation information

LOLER 1998

The Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations 1998 (LOLER) apply to lifting equipment and lifting operations used at work.

Key theatre applications

  • Chain hoists
  • Motorised flying systems
  • Lifting beams
  • Truss systems
  • Suspension points
  • Lifting accessories such as shackles and slings

Main requirements

  • Equipment must be strong and stable.
  • Safe Working Loads must be clearly marked.
  • Lifting operations must be properly planned.
  • Equipment must be thoroughly examined by a competent person.
  • Defects must be recorded and acted upon.

Theatre considerations

Entertainment environments often involve dynamic loading, overhead suspension and public safety risks. Inspection, maintenance and competent operation are critical.

PUWER 1998

The Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations 1998 (PUWER) apply to work equipment used in the workplace.

Key theatre applications

  • Curtain tracks
  • Winches
  • Motor controllers
  • Access equipment
  • Portable tools
  • Powered stage machinery

Main requirements

  • Equipment must be suitable for use.
  • Equipment must be maintained in a safe condition.
  • Users must receive information and training.
  • Dangerous parts must be controlled.
  • Inspections must be carried out where deterioration could create risk.

Theatre considerations

PUWER applies broadly across entertainment venues and often overlaps with LOLER where lifting equipment is involved.

BS EN 17206

BS EN 17206 is the European entertainment technology standard for machinery used in stages and production areas.

Key theatre applications

  • Stage machinery
  • Motorised flying systems
  • Integrated control systems
  • Automated scenery movement
  • Load-bearing entertainment equipment

Main requirements

  • Risk reduction and safety functions.
  • Control system safety.
  • Prevention of uncontrolled movement.
  • Testing and commissioning.
  • Documentation and inspection requirements.

Theatre considerations

BS EN 17206 is highly relevant to modern performance venues using automated or powered entertainment machinery.

ABTT Technical Standards

ABTT Technical Standards provide recognised UK theatre-industry good practice guidance for places of entertainment.

Key theatre applications

  • Theatre infrastructure
  • Suspension systems
  • Inspection and maintenance procedures
  • Operational safety management
  • Entertainment rigging practice

Main principles

  • Use competent persons.
  • Maintain inspection records.
  • Follow recognised industry practice.
  • Ensure safe operation and maintenance.
  • Consider venue-specific risks.

Theatre considerations

ABTT guidance complements legal duties such as LOLER and PUWER and helps interpret good practice within entertainment environments.
